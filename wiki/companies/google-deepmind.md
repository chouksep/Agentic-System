# Google DeepMind

## Overview
Google DeepMind (legal name: DeepMind Technologies Limited) is a British-American artificial intelligence research laboratory and subsidiary of Alphabet Inc., headquartered in London, England. Founded in 2010 by Demis Hassabis, Shane Legg, and Mustafa Suleyman, it was acquired by Google in 2014 and merged with Google Brain in April 2023 to form Google DeepMind. It is responsible for developing Google's Gemini family of large language models and a range of landmark AI research systems including AlphaFold, AlphaGo, and AlphaZero.

## Products & Services
- [[product:gemini]] — Google's family of large language models
- Veo — text-to-video model
- Imagen — text-to-image model
- Lyria — text-to-music model
- AlphaFold — protein structure prediction system
- AlphaGo / AlphaGo Zero / AlphaZero — game-playing AI systems (Go, chess, shogi)
- MuZero — mastered Go, chess, shogi, and Atari 2600 games without domain knowledge
- AlphaGeometry — geometry problem-solving model
- AlphaEvolve, AlphaDev, AlphaTensor — algorithm discovery models
- AlphaStar — StarCraft II AI
- Agent57 — surpasses human performance on all 57 Atari 2600 games
- DeepNash — Stratego-playing reinforcement learning system
- Streams — clinical task management app (co-developed with Royal Free NHS Trust, 2015)

## Competitive Position
- Primary competitor to [[company:openai]] in frontier AI research and large language model development
- The April 2023 merger with Google Brain was explicitly described as accelerating AI work in response to OpenAI's [[product:chatgpt]]
- Operates research centres in the United States, Canada, France, Germany, and Switzerland in addition to London HQ
- As of 2020, had published over 1,000 research papers, including 13 accepted by Nature or Science
- Received "Company of the Year" award from Cambridge Computer Laboratory in 2014
- AlphaFold's protein structure database (200M+ structures) represents a major scientific contribution with no direct commercial competitor at scale
- Maintains an AI ethics board (established post-Google acquisition); membership has not been publicly disclosed
- Operates DeepMind Ethics and Society unit, with philosopher Nick Bostrom as an adviser

## Recent Developments
- 2024-03-01: Mustafa Suleyman appointed EVP and CEO of Microsoft AI
- 2023-04-01: DeepMind merged with Google Brain to form Google DeepMind, in response to competitive pressure from OpenAI's ChatGPT
- 2022-07-01: Announced AlphaFold database release of 200M+ predicted protein structures covering virtually all known proteins
- 2020-01-01: Published Agent57, surpassing human performance on all 57 Atari 2600 games; also made significant advances in protein folding with AlphaFold
- 2019-12-01: Co-founder Mustafa Suleyman announced departure from DeepMind to join Google in a policy role
- 2019-01-01: Released MuZero, mastering Go, chess, shogi, and Atari games without domain knowledge or rules
- 2018-07-01: Trained system to play Quake III Arena
- 2017-10-01: Launched new research team to investigate AI ethics
- 2017-01-01: AlphaGo Zero defeated AlphaGo 100-0; AlphaZero gained superhuman abilities at chess and shogi
- 2016-03-01: AlphaGo beat Lee Sedol (9-dan professional) 4-1 in a five-game match; featured in documentary "AlphaGo"
- 2015-10-01: AlphaGo beat European Go champion Fan Hui 5-0 — first AI to defeat a professional Go player
- 2015-09-01: DeepMind and Royal Free NHS Trust signed information-sharing agreement to co-develop Streams app
- 2014-01-26: Google confirmed acquisition of DeepMind for $400M–$650M
- 2014-01-01: Received "Company of the Year" award from Cambridge Computer Laboratory; introduced neural Turing machines
- 2013-01-01: Published research on AI surpassing human performance in Pong, Breakout, Enduro, and other Atari games
- 2010-11-15: Officially launched by Demis Hassabis, Shane Legg, and Mustafa Suleyman
