# OpenAI

## Overview
OpenAI is an American artificial intelligence research organization consisting of a for-profit public benefit corporation (PBC) — OpenAI Group PBC — and a nonprofit foundation (OpenAI Foundation), headquartered in San Francisco, California. It develops large language models, text-to-image models, and text-to-video models, and its release of ChatGPT in November 2022 has been credited with catalyzing widespread interest in generative AI. OpenAI's stated mission is ensuring that artificial general intelligence (AGI) "benefits all of humanity."

## Products & Services
- [[product:chatgpt]] — conversational AI assistant, released November 2022
- GPT family of large language models (including GPT-5.4)
- [[product:dall-e]] — DALL-E series of text-to-image models
- Sora — series of text-to-video models
- OpenAI Codex — coding assistant
- GPT Image
- Deep Research
- ChatGPT agent
- ChatGPT Atlas
- ChatGPT Health
- OpenAI Gym — platform for reinforcement learning research (released April 2016)
- Universe — software platform for measuring and training AI general intelligence (released December 2016)

## Competitive Position
- Competes in the AI research and commercial AI space; key differentiator is the GPT model family and ChatGPT platform
- Microsoft is both a major investor (27% stake) and cloud infrastructure provider (Azure)
- OpenAI systems run on an Azure-based supercomputing platform
- Faced multiple lawsuits in 2023–2024 for alleged copyright infringement related to training data
- Throughout 2024, roughly half of then-employed AI safety researchers left the company, citing deprioritization of safety goals
- Key competitor: [[company:anthropic]] — founded in 2021 by former OpenAI employees (including ex-VP of Research Dario Amodei) who departed due to directional differences; in 2024, OpenAI also lost Jan Leike, John Schulman, and Durk Kingma to Anthropic
- Key competitor: [[company:google-deepmind]] — Google's AI research lab; the April 2023 merger of DeepMind and Google Brain into Google DeepMind was explicitly triggered by competitive pressure from ChatGPT
- OpenAI received a $200 million U.S. DoD contract for AI in the military in July 2025 (alongside Anthropic, Google, and xAI)

## Corporate Structure
- **Founded**: December 8, 2015 as nonprofit OpenAI, Inc. in Delaware
- **2019**: Transitioned to "capped" for-profit structure (profit capped at 100× any investment); created subsidiaries OpenAI LP, OpenAI GP LLC, and OpenAI Global, LLC; services migrated to Microsoft Azure
- **2025**: Converted to Delaware-based public benefit corporation (PBC); nonprofit renamed OpenAI Foundation (holds 26% stake); for-profit entity renamed OpenAI Group PBC
- All board members of OpenAI Group PBC are appointed by the OpenAI Foundation, which can remove them at any time
- Subsidiaries: OpenAI Global, LLC (PBC); OpenAI Foundation (501(c)(3))
